Audience ANALYSIS

Umbrella Audience Characteristics based off recent WRAL.com user reports & the Triangle Census Report

Age

Approximately middle-aged (WRAL’s main demographic: 25-64); median age in the Triangle area is 37, and 65% of the population is 20-69.

Gender

Male, female or non-binary; WRAL’s demographic is split almost evenly by gender, as is the Triangle area’s population.

Race

59% white, 22% black, 11% hispanic, 5% asian, 2% two or more races, 1% other.

Education

Education is primarily high school graduate and above at 89.7% having received their high school degree. 43.7% have a bachelor’s degree or higher.

Brand Familiarity

Due to WRAL’s focus in the triangle area, being centered in Raleigh, most users will have at least some level of familiarity with WRAL as a brand if not as a website.

Tech Literacy

Any level of computer literacy (some will be more tech-savvy than others).

Empathy interviews

Our group each interviewed one individual who fit within our umbrella audience to get a better understanding of what our users are like. We summarized our findings into charts like the one above to look for trends.

Persona

Guiding Characteristics: White Male, 60, he and his phone do not get along, bachelor’s degree, newspaper sort of guy, old-fashioned.

Style Guide

Color & Font

I chose the font because of its simplicity. This is a news site, there is no need for fancy, decorative fonts that will takeaway from the site's content. Sans-serif fonts are also the easiest to read on screens. I chose colors taken from the North Carolina state flag. This gives a sense of state pride and identity. The color scheme was chosen to be official and the blue creates a tone of trust, something that people look for in news sources.
