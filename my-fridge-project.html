Audience Analysis

While this app is for everyone, the niche that it fits into the most is parents cooking for their family.
‚Äç
It should make meal planning easier and more convenient. It will also add more diversity into their typical meals while making sure they are maximizing their healthy eating options. It is also great for picky eaters and/or dietary restrictions as the recipe algorithm can find a recipe that works for everyone.

Digital Literacy

Very comfortable with tech. Tech is a part of their everyday life, and they already use apps on their phone regularly.

Family status

Geared towards parents who are trying to feed their family and come up with new dinner ideas. Also towards young adults cooking for themselves for the first time.

Age

Anyone who is cooking for themselves / their family. Approx. ~ 22-65

Personas

Improved awareness and confidence in the kitchen

User FLow

Function

1. Item Scanner Allows users to scan in their groceries after they buy them. There is also a manual entry option for products don‚Äôt have a barcode. New items will appear in your virtual fridge and remain there until you mark that it has been eaten. The virtual fridge keeps track of the item‚Äôs nutrition facts ¬†and expiration dates, to ensure that users don‚Äôt let items go to waste. When an item is about to expire users will get a notification and the item will be marked with an exclamation mark. The virtual fridge can be sorted by expiration date or by product type, allowing the user to easily rearrange to their liking.

2. Algorithm SystemThe app will help users easily find recipes based off the items they already have as well as their personal dietary preferences (restrictions, foods they like/don't like). Permanent preferences can be entered into the app through the profile section. The profile section is not limited to just one user, so the whole family can have their own unique profile. The user can also use a wide range of filter options that they can change whenever they want (such as when they are craving something carb heavy or coming up with a recipe for meatless Monday). Users can also click on an item in their virtual fridge to find recipes that use that item.

Style

I wanted to make the app easily usable by all and didn‚Äôt want a complex design to confuse the user and take away from the experience. I used colors that felt fresh and made the user think of food. I chose a crisp, fun, geometric display font for the main title. This adds to the overall design without being too flashy and distracting. For the rest of the text I used a sans-serif font. Sans-serif fonts are also the easiest to read on screens. I chose to use clip art for food items because they can be easily recognizable by all ages and go with the playful branding of the entire design.

30 year old female
