The problem

Brand Pyramid

I wanted the site to come across as feminine, but not "frill". Users will feel empowered and independent after reading and interacting with the site's content.

Competitive analysis

I looked at other sites that were breaking into white space created by the gender gap.

Bumble Bizz: Female Career Advice Blog.
Billie: Women's razor company fighting the "pink tax."
Maven: Women's healthcare resource
Goldie Blox: DIY projects for young girls with a STEM focus.
Girls Who Code: Resources and opportunities for women who want to get involved in tech.

Branding

I wanted the color scheme and logo to come across as feminist without being frilly. IÂ wanted to establish authority wile also using feminine colors.

Color & Font

For the colors I stuck with the primary color (#1542F8) that was provided for the parent financial site that Her's would be apart of. To bring in more of a feminine force I paired the primary blue with a pink and a more subtle red, with muted blue and pink accents. I wanted it to look feminine, but not girly. I wanted the color and font choices to come across as powerful and authoritative so that users felt that they could trust the site's content.

Style Guide

Logo

I wanted the logo to be simple, but still make a statement. I incorporated the blue of the parent site, but used the bold, pink underline to tie in those feminine elements of Her's.
